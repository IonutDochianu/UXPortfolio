Windmill of Goods

In this course we worked on design projects where we had to come up with solutions for specific interface component problems. There were various types, such as an interactive list and navigation menu. In order to select a design pattern that best matches the problem, we learned to first examine it using design principles.

Introduction and my role

The design challenge of this project is to redesign the website of the client, the Dutch Store, in order to shift their brand perception & build their online presence. The Dutch Store is an online webstore that sells Dutch goods, mainly food, abroad in the USA. The website is outdated and needs to be improved for its clients who are mainly Dutch people who miss their culture and food while living abroad in the US. The goal of this project is to create a website design using relevant patterns to make a successful improved web shop of The Dutch Store. In this endavour I had to work paired with one of my classmates. The tasks were divided equally between us, but I was the one mostly in charge of diverging new ideas of redesigning the website. I also was the one that took care of the style guide, the recipe page, which has been a personal addition to the project, and the main product page, whcih can both be seen in the pictures above.

Design process

We were unable to have an interview with the owner of the website, therefore all our research was mainly based on the extensive persona presented to us and on the expert review that me and my colleague were able to do. We were able to go and test the website ourselves, and find the main things that we did not agree with or enjoyed in our experience surfing it. A lot of really old models, of unused buttons, unnecesarry information and badly ordered products. With the information we had, we were able to use the MOSCOW Method to prioritize the most important things we considered needed changing. We started building some of the assets that the website will have, and also constructing the wireframe which we were going to follow. After all the wireframes were built and the assets were discussed with our coach, we then proceeded into building what ended up being the high-fidelity prototype showcased in the pictures shown on the top.

Reflection

We had a list of patterns we knew we had to use to increase the efficiency and user flow of our website before we even began the design process. We discovered during the process that we instinctively include patterns without even realizing it, and we also adjusted some of our preexisting patterns because they no longer seemed to suit. We were able to critically evaluate our design and fix any issues we missed during the design phase with the assistance of the feedback sessions.

We were able to pick up new talents and polish old ones thanks to this initiative. In particular, we delved further into interface design patterns and discovered how to build a system from scratch with key interaction patterns. At addition to providing a new shopping experience in the Dutch Store with a refreshed appearance throughout this project, we also wanted to include new elements like the recipe pages for a better experience.
