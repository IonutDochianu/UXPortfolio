Reading a wave

During this project I had to develop my understanding of the layout of screen elements and grids in interface design, as well as how to effectively structure a website page to show and convey a story.

Introduction and my role

This project was presented to us as an oportunitty to define our knowledge and abilites of using the layout and grids of a screen in order to build more effective interfaces and be able to play with the contents of a website page more easily. The project was presented to us as structuring a given fragment in multiple pages, using the best ways of structuring the content and the layout of the page.

The process

I started by building a really clean first page of the website, which later turned into a scrollable page to the 5 separated parts I had prepared for the fragment. I wanted to havea really simple design on the page to be able to see how I would play with the whole layout in different situations. It was my first time using the grid of the page to its full potential and making sure that every single detail is layed the right way. The escape hatch was the other thing my coach was really keen on having, and so I included the nice icon of the wave which is also present above.

The conclusion

Using the grid and the page layout showed me what a really ordered and knowladgable individual can do even with only having to play with bits of text across a screen. I have also realised how used we are to having an escape hatch and that sometimes was completely out of the scope that I would have. I got so used to having one that I would not even consider having one on my own design, but with this experience I know that it really can build a much smoother and intuitive experience for any user.
