Happynizr

As an aspiring UX Designer, I had to demonstrate my abilities in this project by working alone on a customer assignment. I had to go through the complete design process, resulting in a working high-fidelity prototype, to prove that I was a skilled designer who understood the fundamentals of creation. I had to apply everything I had learnt during my first year of UXD in order to do this.

Introduction and my role

In our last semester of the first year we were all allowed to choose out first ever client to work for. I had been assigned to work for Happynizr. It is a website meant to help businesses with creating their own forms in order to receive feedback. Our job is to work on one of the 4 main problems the website presents: the website, the dashboard, the tool of creating surveys and the connection between the website and the tool. I have chosen to work on the tool. Since the goal of the company is to help business to not have any problem with their surveys they offer a platform for them to build them on, while helping them create and maintain them. The tool mainly focuses on creating surveys and of course all the settings that come with it, so exploring these problems to make an easier life for new users, but also for the employees of Happynizr is my main objective.

Documentation of the process and the creation

For documentation we started by having a big interview with the owner of the company. We had all done research and had been given a demo of the website to play with. After the interview another analyzing of the website has been done, which concluded to starting the preparation of the research metods and questions that would fit the need of the tool. As for participants I was lucky enough to be able to test with people studying business, therefore the future target audience. After a couple user testing, a competitor analysis was also done, and all the results were than placed and analyzed in a Journey map. After that the results and the design vision were carefully built and that is when the prototyping also started.

I wanted to build a prototype that would be as close to the initial one, but which included all the necessary ideas for a better experience. I built a paper prototype first, which was then tested by my friends and family. After that I started building the wireframe and directly into the low-fidelity prototype. I had my classmates test those, and since most of them also knew the problems the website had I was easily able to start working on the high-fidelity prototype presented above.
